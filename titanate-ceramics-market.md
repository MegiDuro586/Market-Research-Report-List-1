<p><p>Titanate ceramics are categorized into two primary market types: oxide and non-oxide. Oxide titanates, primarily composed of titanium dioxide and various metal oxides, exhibit excellent thermal stability and electrical properties, making them suitable for applications in electronics and sensors. Non-oxide titanates, on the other hand, include materials like titanium carbide or titanium nitride, known for their enhanced hardness and chemical resistance, ideal for cutting tools and high-performance coatings. Each type serves distinct industrial needs, enhancing performance across various sectors.</p></p>
