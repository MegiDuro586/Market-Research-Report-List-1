<p><p>Weighting agents are critical in drilling fluids to increase density and control pressure. Barite is the most common agent, providing high density and low costs. Hematite offers a lower volume and enhances filtration properties. Calcium carbonate is used for both weighting and as a bridging agent in certain formations. Siderite and ilmenite serve as alternatives, offering specific density and chemical properties. The "Others" category includes less common materials like gypsum or zeolite, used based on specific drilling conditions and requirements.</p></p>
