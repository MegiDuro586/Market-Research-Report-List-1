<p><p>The wire insulation and jacketing compounds market is divided into two main types: halogenated and non-halogenated plastic compounds. Halogenated plastic wire insulation contains halogens (like chlorine or bromine), providing fire retardancy and reduced smoke emissions, making them suitable for high-risk environments. In contrast, non-halogenated plastic wire insulation lacks these elements, offering a more environmentally friendly option with lower toxicity. This distinction influences their applications in industries ranging from construction to electronics, addressing safety and environmental considerations.</p></p>
