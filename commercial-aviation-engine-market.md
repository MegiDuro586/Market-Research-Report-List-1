Turbojet engines, known for high speeds, are less common in modern passenger aircraft. Turbofan engines, favored for their efficiency and lower noise levels, dominate commercial aviation. Turboprop engines, utilizing propellers for short-haul flights, are efficient at lower speeds. Lastly, turboshaft engines, primarily used in helicopters, provide power through a rotor system. Each type plays a distinct role in optimizing performance, efficiency, and operational costs in aviation.</p></p>
