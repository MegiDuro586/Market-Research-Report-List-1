<p><p>The aircraft superalloy material market comprises three main types: iron-based, cobalt-based, and nickel-based materials. Iron-based superalloys are cost-effective, offering good thermal and mechanical properties, suitable for moderate temperature applications. Cobalt-based alloys excel in oxidation and wear resistance at high temperatures, making them ideal for turbine components. Nickel-based superalloys dominate the aerospace sector due to their exceptional heat resistance, strength, and corrosion resistance at elevated temperatures, essential for gas turbine engines and other critical aircraft components.</p></p>
